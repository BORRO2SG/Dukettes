JMU DUKETTES

The Official Dance Team of James Madison University

About Us

The Dukettes are the official award-winning dance team of James Madison University. They are model representatives of JMU and serve as ambassadors for the university.

The rigorous season as a Dukette begins with summer practices in mid-July, in preparation for the upcoming season. The team attends the National Dance Alliance Collegiate Dance Team camp in August, where they learn various routines and participate in team bonding exercises. Before the school year begins, the Dukettes also attend the Marching Royal Dukes' Band Camp, which takes place the week before classes begin.

During the regular season, the Dukettes practice Monday through Friday, for a minimum of 3 hours each day, which they earn school credit for. The team performs during halftime with Virginia's Finest at each home football game. In addition, they perform at every home basketball game, make various appearances, and travel with the Marching Royal Dukes throughout the fall season. These performances include community, university, national, and international events.

The rigorous and rewarding season culminates in April with a trip to Daytona Beach, Florida, where the nationally acclaimed and award-winning team competes at the National Dance Alliance Collegiate Championship.

The JMU Dukettes hold National Titles in Division I Jazz from 2015, 2016, 2018 and 2021 and are the 2024 DIA Pom National Champions. While it is a huge commitment, being a part of the JMU Dukettes is an unforgettable and rewarding experience.

Meet the Team

Head Coach

Taylor Kim Bailey

Taylor is an alumna of the University of Delaware where she was a four-year member of the nationally ranked UD Dance Team. In 2015, the team brought home two second place wins in Division I Jazz and Hip Hop at the Universal Dance Association Collegiate National Championships. Taylor graduated in 2016 with a B.S. in Management Information Systems and a Concentration in Accounting.

After graduating, Taylor spent the summer as a UDA staff instructor, traveling the country, running camps for high school and middle school dance teams. Taylor currently works as a Project Controller and lives in Harrisonburg with her husband and their two cats.

Awards

2024

In only their second year competing in both the category and the division, the JMU Dukettes earned their FIRST TITLE in DIA Pom. This is the team's 5th national title since 2015.

2021

After a year of challenges stemming from the COVID-19 pandemic and the cancellation of the 2020 NDA Nationals, the Dukettes competed virtually from Harrisonburg and placed first in the Virtual Jazz Division I category. This is the team’s fourth title.

2018

At the 2018 NDA Nationals, the JMU Dukettes placed first in the Division I Jazz category — their third national title in four years.

2016

In 2016, the Dukettes became the NDA National Champions in the Open Division I category for the second year in a row.

2015

For the first time in program history, the Dukettes won first place in Open Division I at the 2015 NDA Collegiate Nationals. JMU also earned their first Top 5 finish in DIA Jazz.
